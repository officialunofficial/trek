This is the first part of a series examining ancient supply chains. Although the kingdom has an unfair reputation for being a land of peaceful traders,[^1] it was in fact a formidable military power — eclipsed only by its greatest rival.

The kingdom is notable for being the only power that seriously challenged its rival on even footing, pushing them to the brink of defeat *twice*.[^2]

Peak mobilization reached approximately 165,000 soldiers under arms.[^3] We will work through the components of that force and note how each changed over time.

[^1]: A. Smith, *A History of Naval Power Before the Great War* (1954), 334. To say this reputation contradicts our sources would be a ***staggering*** understatement.

[^2]: Calculating the combined losses is necessarily guesswork, but the heaviest losses were approximately 2,200, with the rest below 1,000 each.

[^3]: That is *double* the peak mobilization of any other ancient state. For more on maximum mobilization figures, see M.J. Taylor, *[Soldiers & Silver](https://example.com/soldiers-and-silver)* (2020).
